Leon Nichols, Potter

Starting in college, he has over 50 years of experience throwing pottery, building wheels and kilns as a part-time passion.

After many years demonstrating for events in the USA and Canada, he began teaching full-time in Charlotte, NC.

With his electrical background, he also services kilns and other equipment.

About

Known for making large pieces, Leon has been working with clay since 1970 when he built his first kick-style potter's wheel while earning a bachelor's degree in psychology in college. His psychology professor encouraged him by letting him pour a concrete flywheel in their garage. Next, the art professor was encouraging by allowing him to keep his handmade potter's wheel in the sculpture studio and offered a bin of wet clay to use. Leon then was able to obtain firebrick from a nearby glass factory and he built a small kiln on campus. The school bought him a burner and connected the kiln to a gas line.

Upon graduating in 1971, Leon was accepted for a three-month Spring Concentration pottery course at Penland School of Crafts in North Carolina in 1972. Following that, Leon became a full-time potter.

In 1982, Leon put pottery on “the back burner” and worked part-time in making and teaching pottery. He became employed at a small company installing avionics boxes into small aircraft with a goal of repairing avionics with Piedmont Airlines. After 20 months, he was hired by the airline and repaired electronics in the avionics shop until he transferred into the flight simulator department to repair the simulators. He retired from the airline in 2005.

Now Leon teaches eight classes a week in Charlotte, NC as well as repairs pottery equipment and makes pottery.
